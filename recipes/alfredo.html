Shrimp Alfredo

shrimp Alredo with parsely

A popular dish to make when you are in an Italian mood is the famous Alfredo. The dish was created by Alfredo di Leilo in the 1900s. Typically white cream is added to the dish to give it the impeccable flavor it's known for. The shrimp adds extra flavor, that goes with the creamy meal. If you follow the recipe I provided you can this yourself within 30 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • 12 ounces dried fettuccine
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3/4 pund large shrimp
  • Freshly-ground pepper
  • 1 large garlic clove
  • 1 1/2 cups parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parseley leaves
  1. Whisk together the heavy cream, egg yolk, and nutmeg in a bowl
  2. Boil a large pot of water
  3. Once boiling, season with a little bit of salt and add the fettuccine
  4. Sitr and cook according to the package directions
  5. Drain pasta, reserving 1/2 cup of pasta water
  6. Heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the butter and let it melt
  7. Add the shrimp and cooking without moving just untill pink on the bottom(2 to 3 mins)
  8. Season with salt and pepper
  9. Flip the shrimp and add the garlic, cook another 2 minutes
  10. Remove the shrimp to a plate leaving the garlic and butter
  11. Whisk in the heavy cream and egg yolk. Cook untill hot but not boiling.
  12. Once the pasta is almost done, return heat to medium and add parmesan
  13. Cook and sitr untill slightly thicken
  14. Once the sauce is thickened, add the pasta to the sauce and toss untill the sauce is coated in the noodles
  15. Add shrimp back to the pan and toss
